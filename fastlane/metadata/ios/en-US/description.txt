Bluffing card game set in a satirical modern-India corporate-political underworld. Claim roles you don't hold. Call bluffs you're not sure about. Steal, scheme, eliminate — in Hinglish.

Every player hides two role cards. On your turn, claim any role — whether you hold it or not. If an opponent calls your bluff, cards flip. Losers lose influence. Last player standing wins the Gaddi.

Five roles, five archetypes of desi power:
• Netaji Vachan (Neta) — The Eternal Candidate. Made entirely of promises.
• Bhai Teja (Bhai) — Owns the Silence. Quiet, slow, unbothered.
• Babu Filewala (Babu) — Approver of Nothing. Power through the comma.
• Jugaadu Chhotu (Jugaadu) — The Fix-It Man. Solutions mostly illegal.
• Vakil Loophole (Vakil) — The Silver Tongue. Knows three exceptions to every rule.

Ten bot personas, each bluffs differently. Netaji claims his role even when holding Bhai. Bhai Teja goes silent before he strikes. Babu Filewala taxis obsessively. Jugaadu Chhotu does something unpredictable every time. Vakil Loophole challenges on principle. Then there's Didi (vengeance is her love language), Madam Ji (always two moves ahead), Sharmaji's Son (learns nothing), Inspector Reddy (by-the-book, for the right bribe), Startup Bro (pivots every round).

Modes:
• vs AI — 1 human vs up to 9 bot personas
• GAUNTLET (Tarakki ki Seedhi) — 5-rung escalating ladder to Grandmaster
• Team Khel — faction play; allies are never legal targets
• DARBAR — bots chat, form alliances, spread rumours, hold grudges. You can manipulate them.
• TAMASHA — AI plays all seats. Watch it unfold.

DARBAR runs four story arcs at your table: GATHBANDHAN (two bots ally, one eventually defects), AFWAAH (a rumour about someone's role spreads, might be true), STING (a bot leaks a real card claim, now everyone is watching), BADLA (a bot you hit declares a grudge and follows through).

Also: interactive Pehli Hazri tutorial, career stats and bot H2H records, auto-resume via deterministic replay, full VoiceOver support, CVD-safe Okabe-Ito role colours. Plays completely offline — no account, no ads, no server needed.
